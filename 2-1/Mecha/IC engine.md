### **1. Heat & Work Concepts**
**(Pages 2-4)**

*   **Conversion Rule:**
    *   Work $\rightarrow$ Heat: $100\%$ efficiency possible.
    *   Heat $\rightarrow$ Work: $< 100\%$ efficiency (complete conversion impossible).
*   **Engine Definition:**
    *   Device converting **Heat Energy** $\rightarrow$ **Mechanical Work**.

---

### **2. Engine Classifications**
**(Pages 5-6)**

**A. By Combustion Location:**
*   **IC (Internal Combustion):** Combustion *inside* cylinder.
    *   *Ex:* Petrol, Diesel, Gas engines.
*   **EC (External Combustion):** Combustion *outside* cylinder.
    *   *Ex:* Steam engine, Steam turbine.

**B. By Cycle of Operation:**
*   **Otto Cycle:** Spark Ignition (Petrol/Constant Volume).
*   **Diesel Cycle:** Compression Ignition (Diesel/Constant Pressure).
*   **Dual Cycle:** Combination.

**C. Other Criteria:**
*   **Fuel:** Petrol, Diesel, Gas.
*   **Method:** SI (Spark Ignition) vs. CI (Compression Ignition).

### **4. Four-Stroke Petrol Engine (SI)**
**(Pages 8-12)**

*   **Fuel:** Petrol (Gasoline).
*   **Ignition:** Spark Plug.
*   **Cycle Ratio:**
    *   $$1 \text{ Cycle} = 4 \text{ Strokes} = 2 \text{ Crank Revolutions} (720^\circ)$$
    *   $$1 \text{ Stroke} = 180^\circ \text{ rotation}$$

**Operation Sequence:**

| Stroke | Valve Position | Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Suction** | Inlet: **OPEN** <br> Exhaust: **CLOSED** | Air-Fuel mixture drawn into cylinder. |
| **2. Compression** | Both: **CLOSED** | Mixture compressed. Piston moves BDC $\rightarrow$ TDC. |
| **3. Power/Exp.** | Both: **CLOSED** | Spark fires. Gas expands. Work done. |
| **4. Exhaust** | Inlet: **CLOSED** <br> Exhaust: **OPEN** | Burnt gases expelled. |

---

### **5. Four-Stroke Diesel Engine (CI)**
**(Pages 15-18)**

*   **Principle:** Uses high pressure/temperature to self-ignite fuel.
*   **Stroke Definition:** Single traverse of cylinder (TDC $\leftrightarrow$ BDC).

**Operation Sequence:**

1.  **Intake Stroke:**
    *   Intake valve Open.
    *   **Air Only** drawn in.
2.  **Compression Stroke:**
    *   Valves Shut.
    *   Air compressed $\rightarrow$ Temp & Pressure $\uparrow$.
3.  **Power Stroke:**
    *   Valves Shut.
    *   Fuel injected $\rightarrow$ Ignites due to hot air.
    *   Piston forced TDC $\rightarrow$ BDC.
4.  **Exhaust Stroke:**
    *   Exhaust valve Open.
    *   Gases expelled.

---

### **6. Ignition Types**
**(Page 22)**

*   **Petrol Engine:** Spark Ignition (SI).
*   **Diesel Engine:** Compression Ignition (CI).

---

### **7. Two-Stroke Petrol Engine**
**(Pages 23-27)**

**Construction Differences:**
*   No Valves $\rightarrow$ Uses **Ports** (Inlet, Exhaust, Transfer).
*   Ports controlled by **Piston** movement.
*   **Piston Crown:** Deflected shape to direct airflow.

**Working Cycle (2 Strokes / 1 Revolution):**

*   **Stroke 1: Upward (Compression + Inductance)**
    *   Piston moves BDC $\rightarrow$ TDC.
    *   Transfer/Exhaust ports covered.
    *   Mixture above piston: **Compressed**.
    *   Inlet port uncovers $\rightarrow$ Fresh mixture enters **Crankcase**.
    *   Ignition occurs near TDC.

*   **Stroke 2: Downward (Expansion + Exhaust)**
    *   Gases expand $\rightarrow$ Power.
    *   Piston moves TDC $\rightarrow$ BDC.
    *   Mixture in Crankcase: **Partially Compressed** (*Crankcase Compression*).
    *   **Exhaust Port Opens:** Burnt gases exit.
    *   **Transfer Port Opens:** Fresh charge flows Crankcase $\rightarrow$ Cylinder.
    *   *Note:* Deflector on piston prevents fresh charge escaping with exhaust.

---

### **8. Two-Stroke Diesel Engine**
**(Pages 28-30)**

*   **Cycle:** Compression $\rightarrow$ Power $\rightarrow$ (Intake/Exhaust overlap).
*   **Difference:** Compresses **Air** only; Fuel injected for combustion.

### **2. IC Engine Terminology**
**(Pages 33-36)**

*   **Bore ($d$):** Inside diameter of cylinder ($mm$).
*   **Dead Centers:**
    *   **TDC (Top Dead Center):** Extreme top position (Inner DC).
    *   **BDC (Bottom Dead Center):** Extreme bottom position (Outer DC).
*   **Stroke ($L$):** Distance travelled between TDC and BDC.
*   **Clearance Volume ($V_c$):** Space remaining above piston at TDC.
*   **Swept / Displacement Volume ($V_s$):** Volume generated by piston movement.
    $$V_s = A \times L = \frac{\pi}{4} \times d^2 \times L$$
*   **Total Cylinder Volume ($V$):**
    $$V = V_s + V_c$$
*   **Compression Ratio ($r$):** Ratio of max volume to min volume.
    $$r = \frac{V_{total}}{V_{clearance}} = \frac{V_s + V_c}{V_c}$$

### **7. Working Process: 4-Stroke Engine**
**(Pages 56-61)**

**Definition:** 1 Cycle = 4 distinct piston strokes.

**1. Intake (Suction) Stroke:**
*   **Motion:** TDC $\rightarrow$ BDC.
*   **Valves:** Intake **Open**, Exhaust **Closed**.
*   **Action:** Vacuum created $\rightarrow$ Air-fuel mixture drawn in.

**2. Compression Stroke:**
*   **Motion:** BDC $\rightarrow$ TDC.
*   **Valves:** Both **Closed**.
*   **Action:** Mixture compressed. Preparation for ignition.

**3. Power (Working) Stroke:**
*   **Motion:** TDC $\rightarrow$ BDC.
*   **Valves:** Both **Closed**.
*   **Action:**
    *   Spark fires (Petrol) / Injection (Diesel).
    *   Explosion forces piston down.
    *   **Mechanical Work produced.**
    *   Crankshaft completes $360^{\circ}$ (at start of stroke).

**4. Exhaust Stroke:**
*   **Motion:** BDC $\rightarrow$ TDC.
*   **Valves:** Intake **Closed**, Exhaust **Open**.
*   **Action:** Piston pushes burnt gases out.

### **3. Thermodynamic Cycles**
**(Pages 67, 69)**

**A. Otto Cycle (Petrol / SI)**
*   **(1)$\to$(2):** Isentropic Compression.
*   **(2)$\to$(3):** Constant Volume Heat Addition ($Q_{in}$).
*   **(3)$\to$(4):** Isentropic Expansion (Power).
*   **(4)$\to$(1):** Constant Volume Heat Rejection ($Q_{out}$).

**B. Diesel Cycle (Diesel / CI)**
*   **(1)$\to$(2):** Isentropic Compression.
*   **(2)$\to$(3):** **Constant Pressure** Heat Addition ($Q_{in}$).
*   **(3)$\to$(4):** Isentropic Expansion.
*   **(4)$\to$(1):** Constant Volume Heat Rejection.

### **5. Comparison: 2-Stroke vs. 4-Stroke**
**(Pages 78-79)**

**2-Stroke Advantages:**
*   **Power:** $2\times$ power strokes compared to 4-stroke (1 per rev).
*   **Weight:** Lighter, less bulky.
*   **Design:** Simpler (no valves/camshaft), low initial cost.
*   **Efficiency:** Higher *mechanical* efficiency (fewer moving parts).

**2-Stroke Disadvantages:**
*   **Efficiency:** Lower *thermal* efficiency.
*   **Scavenging:** Poor (fuel loss with exhaust).
*   **Wear:** Higher wear & tear.
*   **Lubrication:** High oil consumption.
*   **Noise:** Noisier operation.

### **6. Valve Timing Diagrams**
**(Pages 80, 82, 84)**

*   **Theoretical:** Valves open/close exactly at Dead Centers (TDC/BDC).
*   **Actual (Practical):** Valves open early and close late to improve breathing (Inertia effects).

**Typical Actual Timing (Diesel Example - Page 84):**
*   **IVO (Inlet Valve Open):** $10^{\circ}-20^{\circ}$ *before* TDC.
*   **IVC (Inlet Valve Close):** $25^{\circ}-40^{\circ}$ *after* BDC.
*   **FVO (Fuel Valve Open):** $10^{\circ}-15^{\circ}$ *before* TDC (Injection).
*   **EVO (Exhaust Valve Open):** $39^{\circ}-50^{\circ}$ *before* BDC.
*   **EVC (Exhaust Valve Close):** $10^{\circ}-15^{\circ}$ *after* TDC.
